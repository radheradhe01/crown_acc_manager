Accounting Software Documentation
This document outlines the key features and functionalities required to build a comprehensive accounting software solution. The software aims to provide robust financial management capabilities for businesses, with a focus on ease of use and clear reporting.
1. Company Management
The software will support multi-company management, allowing users to handle the financial records of multiple entities within a single application.
1.1 Company Setup
Initial Company Creation: Upon first use, the system will prompt the user to set up their primary company, including essential details like:
Company Name
Legal Entity Type
Tax Identification Number (TIN) / Employer Identification Number (EIN)
Registered Address
Default Currency
Fiscal Year Start/End Dates
Contact Information (Email, Phone)
Adding Multiple Companies: Users will have the option to add additional companies, each with its own independent set of financial data.
1.2 Company Swapping
Seamless Switching: A prominent and easily accessible option will be available to switch between different registered companies. This ensures that users can manage multiple businesses efficiently without logging in and out.
Contextual Data: All data displayed and operations performed will be contextual to the currently selected company.
2. Customer and Vendor Management
This section details the management of business relationships with customers and vendors, including their initial financial standing and payment terms.
2.1 Customer List
Customer Profiles: Maintain a comprehensive list of all customers, including:
Customer Name (Company/Individual)
Contact Person
Billing Address
Shipping Address
Contact Information (Email, Phone)
Unique Customer ID (auto-generated)
Payment Terms: For each customer, the system will allow the definition of standard payment terms. This is crucial for calculating due dates and identifying overdue invoices. Examples of payment terms include:
Net 30 (payment due 30 days from invoice date)
Net 60
Due on Receipt
15 EOM (15th of the month following the end of month)
2.2 Vendor List
Vendor Profiles: Maintain a comprehensive list of all vendors, including:
Vendor Name (Company/Individual)
Contact Person
Billing Address
Contact Information (Email, Phone)
Unique Vendor ID (auto-generated)
Default Payment Method (e.g., Bank Transfer, Check)
2.3 Opening Balances
Initial Financial Position: The system will provide a mechanism to record the opening balance for each customer and vendor.
Customers: An opening balance represents the amount owed to the company by the customer at the start of using the software.
Vendors: An opening balance represents the amount owed by the company to the vendor at the start of using the software.
Date Specificity: Opening balances should be recorded with a specific effective date.
3. Transaction Management
This section covers the core functionalities for recording financial transactions, including payments, revenue, and costs.
3.1 Bank Statement Upload and Payment Reconciliation
Automated Payment Entry: The system will allow users to upload bank statements (e.g., in CSV, OFX, or QIF format).
Intelligent Matching: Based on the uploaded statement, the system will attempt to automatically match transactions to existing customer invoices or vendor bills.
Manual Allocation: For unmatched transactions, users will have the option to manually allocate payments:
Customer Payments: Apply incoming payments to specific customer invoices or record them as unapplied customer payments.
Vendor Payments: Apply outgoing payments to specific vendor bills or record them as unapplied vendor payments.
Categorization of Other Expenses: Any transactions in the bank statement that are not customer or vendor payments will need to be categorized into appropriate expense accounts (e.g., rent, utilities, salaries).
3.2 Revenue Upload
Revenue Entry: A dedicated module will allow users to record revenue. This could involve:
Invoice Creation: Generating and sending invoices to customers.
Direct Revenue Entry: For cash sales or other direct revenue streams not tied to an invoice.
Integration with Customers: Revenue entries should be linked to specific customers where applicable.
3.3 Cost Upload
Cost Entry: A dedicated module will allow users to record various costs and expenses. This could involve:
Bill Entry: Recording bills received from vendors.
Direct Expense Entry: For immediate expenses not tied to a vendor bill (e.g., petty cash expenses).
Integration with Vendors: Cost entries should be linked to specific vendors where applicable.
4. Reporting
Robust reporting is essential for financial insights. This section outlines the required reports.
4.1 Customer Outstanding Balance Report
Detailed Breakdown: This report will provide a clear overview of amounts due from each customer. It will categorize balances based on payment terms:
Current Due: Amounts not yet due.
Overdue: Amounts past their due date, potentially broken down into aging buckets (e.g., 1-30 days overdue, 31-60 days overdue, etc.).
Total Due: Sum of current and overdue amounts.
Filtering and Sorting: Options to filter by customer, date range, and sort by amount or due date.
4.2 Accounting Report (General Ledger / Trial Balance)
Core Financial Statement: This report will provide a summary of all financial transactions, typically in the form of a General Ledger or Trial Balance.
General Ledger: A detailed record of all transactions for each individual account (e.g., Cash, Accounts Receivable, Sales Revenue, Rent Expense).
Trial Balance: A summary of all ledger accounts with their debit or credit balances, ensuring that total debits equal total credits.
Date Range Selection: Ability to generate the report for specific accounting periods (e.g., monthly, quarterly, annually).
4.3 Other Category Expenses Report
Non-Customer/Vendor Expenses: This report will list all expenses categorized directly from bank statement uploads that are not tied to specific customers or vendors.
Categorization: Expenses will be grouped by their assigned categories (e.g., Office Supplies, Utilities, Travel, Miscellaneous Expenses).
Filtering: Options to filter by expense category, date range, and amount.
